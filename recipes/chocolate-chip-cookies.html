Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

These chocolate chip cookies are the ultimate indulgence, with a perfect blend of textures and flavors. They have crispy, golden-brown edges that give way to soft, chewy centers, creating a satisfying contrast in every bite. The rich, buttery dough is balanced by the sweetness of brown and granulated sugar, while the vanilla adds a subtle warmth. Bursting with gooey pockets of melted chocolate chips, each cookie offers an irresistible combination of sweetness and richness. If you include nuts, they add a delightful crunch, but the cookies are equally divine on their own. They're everything you could want in a chocolate chip cookie — comforting, nostalgic, and utterly delicious.

Ingridients

24-30 cookies
  • 250 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 200 g brown sugar (light or dark, depending on preference)
  • 100 g granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract (10 ml)
  • 375 g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda (5 g)
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder (2.5 g)
  • 1/2 tsp salt (2.5 g)
  • 300 g chocolate chips or chopped chocolate (dark or semi-sweet)

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Line baking trays with parchment paper or silicone baking mats.
  2. Cream Butter and Sugars: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy (this takes about 3–4 minutes using an electric mixer).
  3. Add Eggs and Vanilla: Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each one is fully incorporated. Add the vanilla extract and mix well.
  4. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing just until combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  6. Add Chocolate Chips: Stir in the chocolate chips (and nuts, if using) until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  7. Scoop and Shape: Using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop mounds of dough onto the prepared baking trays, spacing them about 5 cm apart. For a thicker cookie, slightly chill the dough for about 20 minutes before baking.
  8. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 10–12 minutes or until the edges are golden brown and the centers are just set. For a softer texture, remove the cookies when they still look slightly underbaked in the center; they will continue to firm up as they cool.
  9. Cool: Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
